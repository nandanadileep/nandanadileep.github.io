Why 6983 Behaves Exactly Like 53

I was bored and had no phone with me, so pardon me.

I noticed something weird about digit sums of cubes:

1³ = 1→ 1
2³ = 8→ 8
3³ = 27→ 9
4³ = 64→ 1
5³ = 125→ 8
6³ = 216→ 9
7³ = 343→ 1
8³ = 512→ 8
9³ = 729→ 9
10³ = 1,000→ 1

Only 1, 8, 9. Over and over. Why would cubes be so picky?

When I looked into it, I realized I shouldn't be surprised — something obvious is happening.

Every number is built from powers of 10. Like 347 = 3×100 + 4×10 + 7. But in the world of remainders mod 9, the number 10 behaves exactly like 1. So does 100, 1000, 10000...

That means 347 mod 9 acts like 3 + 4 + 7 mod 9. Which is just the digit sum. Adding digits is literally just taking remainders mod 9.

347 % 9= 5
3 + 4 + 7 = 14 → 1 + 4= 5

So what do cubes produce mod 9? Every number is either a multiple of 3, or it isn't. If n = 3k, then n³ = 27k³ — that's 0 mod 9. For everything else — 1, 2, 4, 5, 7, 8 — their cubes mod 9 cycle through just 1 and 8.

Cubes don't choose 0, 1, 8. They have no other option.

Now take 698. Its digit sum is 6 + 9 + 8 = 23 → 2 + 3 = 5. So 698 behaves like 5 mod 9. Which means 698³ behaves exactly like 5³.

Try any number. Same thing.
